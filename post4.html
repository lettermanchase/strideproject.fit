Bypassing the Burnout: Finding Time to Train

Entry #4 | May 5, 2026

There are few things harder than dragging yourself to train after a grueling workday. I’m no stranger to these roadblocks, and it took a lot of trial and error to figure out how to bypass them. In the military, the default answer is usually just abusing caffeine. But after relying on that crutch, I realized it isn't a sustainable strategy—there are much healthier, more effective ways to keep your engine running.

If you want to stop skipping your workouts, you have to attack the problem from two fronts: fixing your energy and hacking your schedule.

Phase 1: Defeating Late-Day Fatigue

You can't train if your tank is empty by 1700. Here is how you keep your energy levels stable throughout the day:

  • The Sleep Baseline: This is non-negotiable. Without 7-9 hours of quality sleep, your baseline fatigue will be too high, and your motivation will be zero. Sleep is your primary recovery tool.
  • Fueling, Not Just Eating: A lot of people make the mistake of skipping breakfast or eating heavy, junk-filled lunches. If you feed your body garbage at noon, your energy will crash by the afternoon. Eat a balanced diet, avoid overeating in the middle of the day, and give your body actual fuel to train later.
  • Combat Dehydration: Dehydration is a stealthy contributor to afternoon lethargy. Drink water consistently to keep your cognitive and physical energy up.
  • Micro-Dose Activity: Don't sit completely still for 8 hours. Incorporate short bursts of activity throughout the day—knock out some pushups during a free moment, stretch, or just walk around your workspace to keep the blood flowing.

Phase 2: Manufacturing Time

Even with your energy fixed, you still have to find the time. If you wait for the "perfect" hour to open up, you will never train.

  • The Written Protocol: Treat your workout like a military tasking. Plan it in advance and write it down. Turning a vague idea into a concrete to-do list drastically increases the chances that you’ll actually execute it.
  • Exploit Hidden Windows: Make exercise a priority by identifying hidden opportunities in your schedule. Waiting for a meeting to start? Doing a lunch break walk? You can fit movement into the margins of your day.
  • Location Independence: Adapt your environment. You don't always need a gym. Train in your living room, hit a local park, or even do desk mobility exercises in your office.
  • Time-Efficient Intensity: You don't need a two-hour gym session to get results. Implement short, intense, time-efficient training methods. A brutal 15-minute circuit is infinitely better than doing nothing at all.
The Field-Tested Verdict: Staying consistent with regular training—even when it's imperfect or squeezed into a 15-minute window—is what builds an unbreakable routine and ultimately transforms your health. Don't let perfect be the enemy of good.
